About Me

My name is Mia and I am a geoscientist turned data analyst in San Francisco, CA.

I received my BS in the geological sciences at San Diego State University and spent about 3.5 years as a PhD candidate studying geological phenomena at Stanford University.

While I of course honed my techincal skills such as the collection, cleaning, and analysis of data, my time in academia also taught me the importance of collaboration and of developing a diverse skill set.

Since then I have broadened my horizons through learning how to code. freeCodeCamp's front-end web development course (HTML, CSS, and JavaScript) was my first experience with code outside of Matlab. Adding Python and SQL to my toolkit was the natural next step for me as I created data analysis and simple machine learning projects.

Through these projects I am continuing to hone my skills in analyzing and interpreting data. I also intend to improve my existing machine learning projects as well as explore more machine learning techniques and algorithms.

My current goals: improve in statistics and mathematics

Current personal project: webscraping WNBA data in Python using BeautifulSoup4

Check out the project page or my github to learn more about some of my personal projects.
