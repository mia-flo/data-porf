Analysis of the relationship between profit and sales vs discounts for a global office supply company

Data Analysis

Python

Objective:

  1. To explore the impact of discounts on sales and profit for a global office supply retailer
  2. To determine what percentages of discounts were associated with increased profit

Results: Discounts at the 7-20% level are associated with a statistically significant increase in both sales and profit .

Language: Python

Tools:

  • Pandas
  • Numpy
  • Math
  • Seaborn
  • Matplotlib
  • SciPy
  • Scikit-Learn

Methods:

  • Time series analysis of revenue and profit
  • Linear regression to predict profit as a function of sales
  • Probability calculation for a given dollar amount at a specified discount percentage
  • 1 sample t-tests to determine whether the impact of discounted sales were statistically significant

Data set: sourced from kaggle

  • 51,290 records
  • 47 countries
  • 1,590 customers

Analyzing total sales and profit by discount percentage

Count by discount percentage

The business offers a wide range of discount percentages ranging from 7-85%. A surprising number of those discounts are 40% and above. This likely contributes to the low profitability of the business.

Profit by discount percentage

Time series analysis of sales, profit, and discounts

Has the increased number of discounts given correspond to an increase in transactions (orders)?

The number of monthly discounts and orders display similar trends of increasing throughout the year. There also appears to be a cyclical pattern, with spikes in June, September, and November.

Has an increase in the number of unique orders translated to an increase in sales and profit from 2011-2014?

The trend of sales amount per month is similar to that of the number of discounts and orders. However, profit has remained relatively stagnant average sales more than doubling from 2011-2014.

Is the increase in sales associated with discounts statistically significant?

Groups for sales 1 sample t-test

The sales distribution was log normalized prior to the t-test.

Sales t-test results

7-20% discount do indeed lead to an increase in sales overall and in 2014, the year with the highest sales.

Most importantly, are discounts also leading to an increase in profit?

The profit distribution is already approximately normal so no transformation was necessary.

Groups for profit 1 sample t-test

Profit t-test results

7-20% discount do indeed lead to an increase in profit overall and in 2014, the highest sales year.

However, profit has still been stagnant for this business, in part due to the large number of >20% discounts offered.
