Exploratory analysis of global COVID-19 impact from May 2020-May 2023

Objective: to learn SQL and how to use Tableau, the data visualization software

Languages: SQL

Tools: MS SQL Server

Methods: data cleaning, common table expressions, window functions for data aggregation, geographic heat map of vaccinations and deaths , time series analysis of COVID cases and deaths

Results: Globally, the rate of death was around 0.9% for those who contracted COVID. The US was one of the hardest hit countries in terms of total deaths.

Data set: sourced from the World Health Organization

Total global human impact by May 2023

Observing impact on a country level

While the data gathered may not be entirely accurate for every country, we can use heatmaps to make quick, qualitative comparisons between countries to observe the local impact.

Countries with relatively high rates of cases/population include the United States, France, Australia, Iceland, New Zealand, etc.

The United States, however, had the highest number of deaths. And despite a lower rate of cases reported for these countries, Brazil, Russia, India, and Mexico are just some of the countries that experienced a high rate of death.

A higher rate of death may be linked to several other factors such as a patient's overall health prior to contraction, quality of available care at the time, etc.

Total vaccinations

Vaccines started becoming available for select groups in the US at the end of 2020 following select clinical trials.

The percent of individuals who received at least one does of a vaccinane is quite high except for in countries in Africa.

Time series analysis by select county

I chose countries with large populations, some first world, and countries that I have a personal interest in for a more indepth look.

Rolling count - % contracted over time by country

A rolling count of percent of the population who at some point contracted COVID from May 2020-May 2023. By May 2023, it is estimated that France and South Korea reached nearly 60% of the population who at on point contracted COVID.

And while the US had the highest rate of COVID deaths, the percent of the population who contracted it was roughly half that of France and South Korea.

Despite the introduction of vaccines, the US, France, and the UK saw a steady increase in COVID contractions. Even more concerning was the sharp increase observed in several countries during later 2021-early 2022.

This timing seems to coincide with holidays and lockdown restrictions easing up and a greater availability of vaccinations. One would think that higher vaccination would lead to lower rates of contraction, but perhaps more people decided to congregate after receiving the vaccine.

Rate of population infected over time

France and South Korea in particular saw large spikes in population vs # of cases in the earlier parts of 2022.

Several fluctuations were observed for France.

Rate of deaths per cases over time

France and the UK were the early leaders in deaths per cases.

Various conditions vs COVID death rate by country

It is likely that numerous different factors influenced each country's deaths per COVID cases. While difficult to determine the exact factors and their combined impacts on likelyhood of death after contracting COVID, I chose several variables to visualize.

On the y-axis the log value for each country's rate of death (cases/deaths). On the x-axis' are cardiovascular death rate, human development index, population density, and life expectancy.
